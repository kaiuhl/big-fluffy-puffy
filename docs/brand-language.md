# Brand Language

## Position

Big Fluffy Puffy is a nonprofit for fireless camp culture.

The core idea: prepared warmth is better than campfire dependency. BFP should feel capable, funny, outdoors-literate, and unsentimental. The organization is not scolding campers. It is giving them a better identity to step into.

Primary public line:

> Skip the campfire. Pack the warmth.

Mission language:

> Big Fluffy Puffy advocates for a simple shift in outdoor culture: skip the campfire and carry enough warmth to enjoy the night without one.

## Visual Direction

The design language is austere field editorial.

- Black, white, and controlled grayscale.
- Typography first.
- Photos and video should carry the warmth, landscape, fabric, weather, and human feeling.
- Color is rare and functional. Use signal orange for warning, fire, or action.
- Avoid soft outdoorsy cliches: no leafy marks, watercolor forests, beige lifestyle mush, or generic nonprofit optimism.
- Let the page feel like a field notice, gear tag, independent magazine, and certification stamp all sharing one system.

## Typography

Use scale, weight, spacing, and rules before color.

- Body/UI: a serious grotesk sans. Start with system UI; consider Archivo, Inter, or a paid grotesk later.
- Display: heavy, condensed, or blunt when the page needs force.
- Headings should be confident and sparse.
- Body copy should be direct, practical, and unflowery.
- Certification marks can use stamped, numbered, or label-like typography.

## Voice

Public voice:

- Clear.
- Warm.
- Dryly funny.
- Family-safe by default.
- Serious about fire risk without sounding institutional.

Insider voice:

- Sharper.
- More knowing.
- Comfortable with the BFP joke.
- Still never sloppy or mean.

Avoid:

- Guilt-first climate language.
- Vague "protect nature" filler.
- Startup hype.
- Overexplaining the joke.
- Pretending campfires are evil. The point is that the woods do not need every night outside to revolve around one.

## Copy Examples

Short lines:

- The forest does not need your fire.
- You probably do not either.
- Bring the puffy. Leave the matches.
- Fireless is not joyless.
- The vibe survives.

Longer language:

> We are building a culture where skipping the campfire feels prepared, social, and normal. Better insulation, better lighting, better habits, fewer ignition points.

> BFP starts in the Pacific Northwest, where seasonal fire restrictions already change how people camp. Our job is to make that shift feel like belonging, not compliance.

## Design Rules For The Website

- Use black text on off-white or white as the default surface.
- Use white text on black for editorial impact.
- Use hard rules, labels, numbers, and generous negative space.
- Avoid rounded, soft, card-heavy layouts.
- Let future photos and video be large, crisp, and emotionally specific.
- Keep CTAs blunt and rectangular.
- Keep early pages sparse until the organization has real programs to show.
